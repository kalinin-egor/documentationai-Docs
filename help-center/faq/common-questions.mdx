## General

### What is GPUniq?

GPUniq is a GPU rental marketplace that connects users who need compute power with GPU owners worldwide. Rent high-performance GPUs for AI training, machine learning, rendering, and more at competitive prices.

### How is GPUniq different from AWS/GCP/Azure?

GPUniq is a peer-to-peer marketplace:
- **Lower prices**: Up to 70% cheaper than major cloud providers
- **More GPU options**: Access to consumer and professional GPUs
- **Flexible billing**: Per-minute, hourly, or longer-term pricing
- **No commitments**: Rent for as little or as long as you need

### What GPUs are available?

The marketplace includes various NVIDIA GPUs depending on what providers have connected:
- Consumer: RTX 3090, RTX 4090
- Professional: A100, H100, A6000, L40S
- And more depending on provider availability

## Payments

### What payment methods are supported?

- **USDT (TRC20)**: Deposit and withdraw using Tether on Tron network
- **YooKassa**: Bank cards and local methods for Russian users

### How does billing work?

You choose a pricing type when renting:
- **Per minute**: Most flexible, charged every minute
- **Per hour/day/week/month**: Discounted rates for longer commitments

Your balance is deducted in real-time as you use GPU resources.

### Can I withdraw my balance?

Yes, you can withdraw your balance in USDT to any TRC20 wallet address.

## Rentals

### How do I rent a GPU?

1. Browse the marketplace at [gpuniq.com](https://gpuniq.com)
2. Filter by GPU model, VRAM, price, location
3. Click on a GPU to see details
4. Click "Rent Now" and choose pricing type
5. Get instant SSH access

### How do I connect to my rented GPU?

Use SSH with the credentials provided in your dashboard:

```bash
ssh root@{host} -p {port}
# Enter password from dashboard
```

### What if I can't connect?

1. Verify your instance is "Running" in the dashboard
2. Double-check the SSH host, port, and password
3. Try stopping and starting the instance
4. Contact support if issues persist

### How do I stop being charged?

Click "Stop" to pause billing (instance may be preserved) or "Delete" to terminate completely.

## For Providers

### How do I become a GPU provider?

1. Have a machine with NVIDIA GPU(s)
2. Install the GPUniq agent
3. Register your agent via API with pricing
4. Your GPU appears in the marketplace

### What's the commission structure?

GPUniq takes a small platform fee from each rental. Providers receive the majority of the rental price directly to their balance.

### Can I control who rents my GPU?

You can set maximum rental durations and force-stop problematic rentals through the provider dashboard.

## Technical

### Do GPUs come with CUDA installed?

Most providers have CUDA pre-installed. Verify by running `nvidia-smi` after connecting.

### Can I use custom Docker images?

Docker image support is coming soon. Currently, you connect via SSH to the base environment.

### Is my data secure?

Each rental runs in an isolated container. When you delete your instance, data is removed. For sensitive workloads, ensure you delete instances after use.
